about me

Hey guys, its Emma! I’m a fourth year Computer Science student at York University in Toronto, Ontario. I’m currently back in Surrey, BC visiting my family until further notice. Due to quarantine boredom, I thought I would make myself a (somewhat) nice website.

I enjoy sports, weight-lifting, making art and playing the piano. I also play for the Varsity Women's Basketball Team at York!

I started off university at UBC Okanagan. I decided to enrol in a Bachelor of Fine Arts since I didn't know what else to do. I quickly learned that was not the career path for me, so I switched into the Digital Media program. That’s when I took my very first “intro” computer science course and loved it.

After two years of school at UBCO, my life got thrown a huge, last minute curveball and I was forced to transfer. That’s when I found York and decided to move to Toronto. It was the best decision I have ever made! At that point I said screw it and switched into to a BSc. in Computer Science.

Now, I have one more year left to play basketball and three more semesters left of school! I miraculously landed a software developer internship with IBM for the summer and I’m excited for what the future holds.

On the daily, you can find me enjoying a nice episode of Jeopardy, doing a crossword puzzle or listening to Fleetwood Mac.

projects

fridge sensor

A simple program using Arduino, Adafruit.io and IoT. It uses a circuit with a temperature sensor to detect if a fridge has gone above or below its ideal temperature. If the fridge has gone above 7 degrees Celcius, an LED light on the circuit blinks. If it has gone below -1 degree Celcius, the LED light is solid.

Github Repo

ball-paddle game

A simple game using Processing where the objective is to see how many times you can hit the ball off the paddle before missing.

Github Repo

dvd screen

A recreation of the famous DVD screensaver using Processing. The "objective" of this is to see how long it takes until the DVD icon hits one of the corners of the screen. This is a classic way to pass time while reminiscing about your childhood.
